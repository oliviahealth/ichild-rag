uterine rupture what is a uterine rupture a uterine rupture is a tear in the wall of the uterus usually at the spot where an incision was made during a previous C-section a complete rupture is when the tear goes through all layers of the uterus this can be very dangerous for mother and baby most occur during labor but they can also occur during late pregnancy is the common luckily these are rare events especially for women who have never had a C-section or surgery on the uterus uterine rupture is most common in women who try to have a vaginal delivery after having a c-section in the past signs of a uterine rupture ruptures usually happen in labor and there may not be clear signs right away the first indicator is an abnormality in the fetal heart rate other signs include abdominal pain vaginal bleeding rapid pulse referred pain in the chest or slowed or stop to labor how is a uterine rupture treated the baby will be delivered by C-section and the uterus will be repaired the damage is too great a hysterectomy might be needed either way things should be taken easily following surgery be careful getting up get lots of rest eat healthy foods and drink plenty of water
